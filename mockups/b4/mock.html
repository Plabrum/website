On the cost of premature abstractions

Published March 14, 2026 · Updated April 2, 2026
engineering process opinion

Every engineer eventually learns the same lesson twice. The first time, an abstraction saves them. The second time, the same abstraction costs them three weeks. Somewhere between those two events lives the actual craft of the job: knowing which one you're looking at.

§ 1 The trap of three call sites

I used to think the answer was experience — that you simply built enough systems to develop a nose for it. I no longer believe that.

¹ I've worked with two dozen engineers in the last five years and the correlation between tenure and abstraction restraint is essentially zero.
Plenty of senior engineers reach for abstractions before they have any business doing so, and plenty of juniors hold off on them with surprising discipline.

Duplication is far cheaper than the wrong abstraction. — Sandi Metz

§ 2 Naming the duplication

What I've started doing instead is letting the duplication sit, deliberately, with a comment that names it. Something like // duplicated from billing/invoice.ts. That single line buys an enormous amount of clarity.

Three similar things are not a pattern. They're a coincidence with delusions of grandeur. 
tsfunction pricingTier(user: User): Tier {
  if (user.plan === "free") return "free";
  if (user.plan === "team") return "team";
  return "enterprise";
}

The function above has appeared, in some form, in every codebase I've worked in for a decade. It is almost always wrong to centralize it until at least three places need it and at least two of them need exactly the same answer.
